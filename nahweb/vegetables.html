VEGETABLES

Vegetables are edible parts of plants that are consumed by humans as part of a meal. They can include: Leaves (e.g., spinach, lettuce) Roots (e.g., carrots, beets) Stems (e.g., celery, asparagus) Flowers (e.g., broccoli, cauliflower) Fruits used as vegetables (e.g., tomatoes, cucumbers) Seeds (e.g., peas, corn) Vegetables are rich in vitamins, minerals, fiber, and antioxidants, making them essential for a healthy diet. They come in many colors, flavors, and textures and can be eaten raw or cooked in various ways. Eating a variety of vegetables daily supports overall health and helps prevent many diseases.
